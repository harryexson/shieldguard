How to Protect Against Pegasus & Similar Spyware

Advanced surveillance threats like Pegasus exploit system-level vulnerabilities in mobile operating systems. Here's how to defend yourself.

1

Uninstall Unknown Apps

Scan your full application list periodically and delete anything unfamiliar. Surveillance payloads often embed themselves under nondescript names that blend into system menus.

2

Keep Your OS Updated

Turn on automatic OS updates and apply patches without delay. Zero-click spyware relies on known software flaws — staying current closes those doors.

3

Review App Permissions

Audit which apps hold sensitive permissions like camera, microphone, and GPS. If a utility or game doesn't require those capabilities, restrict them immediately.

4

Use ShieldGuard

ShieldGuard's real-time app scanning, network traffic analysis, and domain reputation engine catch surveillance callbacks and block malicious infrastructure automatically.

5

Enable Multi-Factor Authentication

Enable multi-factor authentication across email, financial services, and social platforms. Even if credentials get compromised, MFA blocks unauthorized entry.

6

Watch for Warning Signs

Rapid battery drain, data usage bursts with no clear cause, processes running unseen in the background, or the camera light activating unprompted are common red flags.
